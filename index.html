How to Enter Values

Follow the instructions below to enter values:


  • Temperature: Enter the cow's body temperature in °C. The average body temperature of a healthy cow is between 38.5°C and 39.5°C. A temperature above this range may indicate an infection, fever, or inflammation, which could be a sign of a health problem such as mastitis, pneumonia, or metabolic disorders. Regular monitoring of temperature is crucial to identify early signs of illness.

  • Udder Hardness: Select the hardness of the udder from the dropdown (0 = very soft, 5 = extremely hard). Udder hardness can be an indicator of inflammation or infection, such as mastitis. A very soft udder may suggest poor milk production, while an extremely hard udder often points to a severe infection or blockage. Early detection of changes in udder hardness is vital for timely treatment.

  • Milk Visibility: Select whether milk is visible (1) or not (0). Visible milk is typically a sign of normal lactation. If milk is not visible, it could indicate an issue such as insufficient milk production, blockage in the milk ducts, or other health concerns. If this persists, it may require further examination by a veterinarian to determine the underlying cause.

  • Milk Color: Select the milk color on a scale from 0 to 5. Milk color can provide clues about the cow’s health and nutrition. Normal milk is white, but abnormalities in color can indicate health issues. For example, yellow or greenish milk may indicate mastitis or ketosis, while bloody milk can signal an injury or infection. Monitoring milk color is essential for detecting early signs of disease or malnutrition.
